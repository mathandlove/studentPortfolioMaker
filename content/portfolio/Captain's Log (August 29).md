# Captain's Log (August 29)

ENLP 3000-001

Jordan Miller

Today's conversation with my group is incredibly insightful. We focused on asking some big questions that stemmed from themes discussed in our interview. The question we spent the most time answering was whether academic performance was actually a good measurement of intelligence. We came up with this question after recognizing that in all of our interviews, academic performance and technical intelligence were valued less than other forms like emotional intelligence. We began to answer this question by establishing that academic performance is a better measurement of effort than pure intelligence. Our reasoning was that if a very knowledgeable person does not apply themselves, they will not perform well in school. Additionally, we can expect that a person who has a lower baseline understanding of the given concept will perform highly if they work hard. As a result, we concluded that an intelligent person has the capacity to recognize that they need to work hard. As a result, intelligent people tend to perform well in school, but their intelligence is far more nuanced than that. We also discussed the importance of emotional intelligence in our family. Each of us realized that our families value being emotionally intelligent (whether internally or externally) far more than academically.
