# Captain's Log (August 25)

ENLP 3000-001

Jordan Miller

**Engineering Mythos:**

- Maverick
- Expert
- Macho
- Technophile
- Non-Communicator

These facets are very interesting to me as I reflect on my experience as an engineering student. I still see the relevance of all of these facets in my day-to-day classes. Many of my peers, friends, and colleagues exemplify all of these facets and fit perfectly into this archetype. However, when I look at myself, I do not see all of these facets. This is very revealing because during my time studying engineering, I have never felt like I quite "fit in with the rest of my classmates." When I consider these facts, the reason I feel this way becomes very apparent. While I do exemplify the expert and macho facets, the other three facets are where I feel differently. While my peers are very confident and capable as the maverick, I often need help and support from others. Additionally, I love communicating with others. The non-communicator archetype has been a difficult adjustment for me, as I am constantly hoping to get input from those around me. Human relationships are so so important to me; however, this sentiment has not been shared among my peers.

**Intelligence Mind Mapping:**

Types of intelligence:

- Technical (Book smart, hard skills, training, content comprehension)
- Interpersonal (Communication, confidence, social understanding/recognition, popularity)
- Creativity (Willingness to try, learn, and take risks, confidence in one's own ability to learn)

My intelligence:

1. Creativity: I am confident in my own abilities to pick up new skills, try new things, meet new people, etc., because I am smart enough and brave enough to put myself out there.
2. Interpersonal: I am an outstanding communicator both professionally and casually. I process information by talking about it. However, I sometimes struggle with my ability to develop deep relationships with new people. It takes me a long time to "click" with new groups of people.
3. Technical: I am great at learning new things but my base of technical knowledge often seems lower than my peers. If I want to keep up with them, I am constantly adjusting and picking up necessary skills that I don't already have.
