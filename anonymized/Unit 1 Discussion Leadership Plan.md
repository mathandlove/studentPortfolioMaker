## Discussion Questions (in order of priority)

1. "There was no living soul on Lusitania who really knew Novinha. But in this icy cave in Reykjavik, on the icy world of Trondheim, Ender Wiggin knew her, and loved her, and his eyes filled with tears for her." (Pg 65)

   **Discussion theme:** Leadership empathy (love vs understanding)

   **Goal:** Discuss whether good leaders need to "love" their followers or merely "understand" them.

   - What is the difference between knowing someone and loving someone?
   - Does Ender have the capacity to empathize with someone he has never met before?
   - Do his abilities as a speaker for the dead enhance his ability to have empathy for others?
   - Is Ender's ability to empathize part of what makes him a good leader?

2. "No Ender, it will be exactly as if I died, and you'll know that you're the one who killed me." (Pg 78)

   **Discussion theme:** Leadership empathy (sacrifice of relationships)

   **Goal:** Discuss how leaders make difficult decisions when there are potential negative consequences.

   - Do you agree with Valentine's opinion?
   - Why do leaders often have to make decisions that sacrifice relationships with some to benefit others?
   - How do leaders approach these difficult decisions?

3. "Your people are fools. We know the truth. We know who killed us, and it wasn't you." (Pg 70)

   **Discussion theme:** Leadership responsibility (blame and consequences of decision-making)

   **Goal:** Discuss how leaders accept responsibility for the actions of their team. Consider if Ender was really at fault for the destruction of the buggers.

   - Did Ender kill the buggers? If not, who really did?
   - Should Ender feel responsible if he was manipulated? Why does he still feel responsible?
   - As leaders, how are we influenced by the systems and institutions we lead within?
   - Do we as leaders have an obligation to take responsibility for our actions regardless of the influence of others?

4. "&lt;Let us live again.&gt; 'I'm trying.' Why else do you think I would have wandered from world to world all of these years if not to find a place for you?" (Pg 69)

   **Discussion theme:** Leadership responsibility (reconciliation and setting the record straight)

   **Goal:** Discuss whether leaders have a responsibility to make things right after their decisions have negative impacts.

   - Does Ender have a responsibility to find the Buggers a new home?
   - Does Ender have to commit the rest of his life to setting the record straight? At what point has he given them enough back?
