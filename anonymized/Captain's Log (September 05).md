# Captain's Log (September 05)

ENLP 3000-001

Jordan Miller

Gardner's Multiple Intelligences related closely to my Origins Exploratory Exercise because in my interview with my mother, we discussed that there are more facets to intelligence than simply logical and mathematical. In particular, we identified interpersonal and intrapersonal intelligence as being very important to our family. The only part of our conversation that is not reflected well in Gardner's model is the "ability to learn new things." I called this flexible intelligence in my reflection. Gardner's model does not recognize that being able to learn new things quickly is a form of intelligence. This makes me wonder if flexible intelligence is another type that he may have missed, or whether flexible intelligence is a new level of thinking entirely.

**Bean's Intelligence Through the Lens of Howard Gardner's Hypothesis:**

Interpersonal:

- Bean understands how people feel about him
- Doesn't understand why they feel the way they do
- Bean has little emotional understanding
- Sister Carollata hugs (he hugs her to make her feel better)

Bodily Kinesthetic:

- Bean is small, thus he must compensate with his mind
- When he intentionally eats less in the dining hall, he sacrifices bodily growth to keep his mind sharp

Linguistic:

- Lack of conversation with Petra
- He is vulnerable when students call him out - he is forced to talk
- Conversation with Benzo: He needs to ease Benzo
- When he speaks to Dimach, he recognizes the tension in the conversation
- Intentional timing

Intra-personal:

- He doesn't want others to know anything about himself
- He questions why he would want to climb the vent
- He needs to adjust his thinking from just focusing on survival to thriving
- He can recognize the new environment and adjust
- He recognizes that he doesn't know everything

Spatial:

- Can visualize the different decks of the station
- "Wheel on a cart" analogy
- Figuring out the vent system
- He had a visual map in his head when he was finding the janitor's apartment
- He didn't need to put his hand on the wall
- He can imagine without actually seeing

Naturalist:

- Analysing his surroundings
- Shift from Earth to space
- He understands that he gets nauseous on the spaceship. He can work through the feelings of nausea
- Laws of physics!!!
- Bean doesn't recognize all of the animals on the uniforms

Logical and Mathematical:

- He doesn't do what other students want him to do
- He is an unknown quantity, making him dangerous
- He recognizes that he is being quantified by all of his higher-ups
- He doesn't want the higher-ups to be able to quantify him

Existential:

- Bean asks about the role of the battle school
- He asks why they are there in the first place
- What is the purpose of all of this
- Why are the other kids just listening and following?
- Freedom in his harness on the ship

Musical:

- Recognizes Dimach's steps in the dark
- He recognizes tone in conversation
- When he is mimicking slang
